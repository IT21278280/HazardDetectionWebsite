Autonomous IoT-Enabled Hazard Detection System for Deaf Drivers

This project, developed by Group 24-25J-132 at SLIIT, enhances road safety for deaf drivers in urban environments like Colombo. It integrates horn detection (94.2% accuracy), lipreading (10.8% WER), and driver behavior monitoring (96% accuracy) using IoT and AI technologies, reducing response times by 1.2 seconds.

What Do We Provide?

Precision

Real-time hazard detection with 94.2% accuracy for informed decisions.

Safety

Protects deaf drivers with robust monitoring and alert systems.

Accessibility

Empowers deaf drivers with visual and tactile feedback.

Innovation

Cutting-edge IoT and AI integration for future mobility.

Introduction

Deaf drivers face challenges in urban traffic, especially in Colombo (85 dB noise). This project uses AI and IoT to detect hazards, interpret lip movements, and monitor behavior, launched in September 2024 to enhance inclusivity.

Domain

Literature Survey: Existing systems lack directional awareness (Beritelli [1], Zhao [3]).

Research Gap: No integrated solution for deaf drivers.

Problem: Missed auditory cues in noisy traffic.

Objectives: >90% detection, <12% WER, <200 ms latency.

Methodology: CNNs, TDOA, IoT (ESP32, Raspberry Pi).

Technology: TensorFlow, Flutter, MQTT, Jetson Nano.
