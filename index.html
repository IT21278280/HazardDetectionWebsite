Autonomous IoT-Enabled Hazard Detection System for Deaf Drivers

This project, developed by Group 24-25J-132 at SLIIT, introduces a system to enhance road safety for deaf drivers in urban environments like Colombo. It integrates horn detection (94.2% accuracy), lipreading (10.8% WER), and driver behavior monitoring (96% accuracy) using IoT and AI technologies. Our solution reduces response times by 1.2 seconds, empowering deaf drivers with safer, independent mobility.

Introduction

Deaf drivers face significant challenges in urban traffic, where auditory cues like car horns are critical for safety. In Colombo, with traffic noise levels averaging 85 dB, traditional assistive technologies fall short due to their inability to provide real-time, directional alerts. This project addresses this gap by developing an autonomous IoT-enabled system that uses advanced AI to detect hazards, interpret lip movements, and monitor driver behavior, tailored specifically for the deaf community.

Launched in September 2024, the project aims to bridge accessibility gaps in transportation, aligning with global inclusivity goals. By leveraging cutting-edge technology, we seek to reduce accident risks and enhance the quality of life for deaf individuals in Sri Lanka.

Domain

Tools and Technologies

  • Hardware: ESP32, Raspberry Pi, NVIDIA Jetson Nano
  • AI Frameworks: TensorFlow (for CNNs and lipreading models)
  • Software: Flutter (UI development), MQTT (IoT communication)
  • Algorithms: Time Difference of Arrival (TDOA) for sound localization
  • Development Tools: Git, VS Code, Arduino IDE

These tools enable real-time processing, low-latency alerts, and a user-friendly interface tailored for deaf drivers.

Achievements

  • Achieved 94.2% accuracy in horn detection using CNNs (tested May 2025).
  • Reduced lipreading Word Error Rate to 10.8% with optimized models (April 2025).
  • Improved driver behavior monitoring to 96% accuracy (March 2025).
  • Decreased system response time to 180 ms, surpassing the 200 ms target (May 2025).
  • Presented progress at SLIIT Research Symposium (January 2025).

These milestones reflect our progress toward a robust, reliable solution as of May 23, 2025.
